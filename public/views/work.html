Works

The story of my experiments with truth

"Autobiography: The Story of My Experiments with Truth" is Mahatma Gandhi's autobiographical work that chronicles his life, beliefs, and his development of the principles of nonviolence and Satyagraha (truth force). In this book, Gandhi candidly reflects on his personal experiences, struggles, and experiments that shaped his philosophy and actions. The autobiography is divided into various chapters, each detailing different stages of Gandhi's life, from his childhood and early experiences to his time in South Africa and his involvement in India's struggle for independence. Through his narrative, Gandhi explores his spiritual journey, the challenges he faced, his attempts to live a life in accordance with his principles, and his philosophy of using nonviolent resistance to confront social and political injustices.

India of my Dreams

"Hind Swaraj or Indian Home Rule" is a book written by Mahatma Gandhi in 1909. In this work, Gandhi presents his views on self-governance and the concept of "Swaraj," which means self-rule or home rule. The book is presented in the form of a dialogue between the editor and the reader, where Gandhi articulates his thoughts on various aspects of modern civilization, politics, and the future of India. In "Hind Swaraj," Gandhi criticizes Western civilization and its influence on India, arguing that it has led to moral degradation, materialism, and exploitation. He proposes that true freedom and progress can only be achieved through the revival of Indian values, culture, and self-reliance. Gandhi believes that self-governance should not only apply to political independence but also encompass individual and societal development.

Key to Health

"Key to Health" is a collection of writings by Mahatma Gandhi on health and well-being. In this book, Gandhi discusses his philosophy of health, emphasizing the importance of natural remedies, a balanced lifestyle, and holistic living. He shares his beliefs that physical health is closely linked to mental and spiritual well-being, and he advocates for self-care and self-discipline as crucial components of a healthy life. The book touches on topics such as diet, fasting, exercise, and the need to align one's actions with ethical principles. "Key to Health" provides insights into Gandhi's holistic approach to health and his belief that simple and natural practices can contribute to individual and societal well-being.
